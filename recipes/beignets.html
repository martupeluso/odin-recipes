Princess Tiana's Beignets

Description

These classic New Orleans Beignets are Tiana's specialty! They are soft, pillowy, and dusted with sweet powdered sugar.

Make them from scratch using this easy recipe, and enjoy them with breakfast, brunch, or dessert with a warm mug of coffee!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon active yeast
  • 3 tablespoons + 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 small egg + 1 yolk
  • 1 ½ tablespoons melted unsalted butter
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • 2 cups + 2 ½ tablespoons All-Purpose Flour
  • Neutral oil for frying (such as canola oil)
  • Honey
  • Powdered sugar

Steps

  1. In the bowl of a standing mixer, combine warm water, yeast, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Whisk until combined and let sit for 7-10 minutes until a raft forms on top.
  2. Add remaining sugar, milk, eggs, and melted butter to the bowl. Whisk until combined.
  3. Attach the bowl to the stand mixer with the hook attachment. Add salt and flour. Mix on medium speed until smooth and the dough pulls away from the inside of the bowl, about 3-6 minutes. (If making by hand, knead the dough in a large bowl until smooth.)
  4. Grease the bowl a little and cover the dough with plastic wrap and a kitchen towel. Let it sit in a warm place until it doubles in size, about an hour.
  5. Transfer the dough to a floured surface. Roll out until ½” (1.2cm) thick. Cut into 1 ½” to 2” (8cm) squares or rectangles. Transfer uncooked beignets to a tray lined with parchment paper dusted with flour to prevent sticking.
  6. Using a steel spatula, add 5-6 beignets to a pot of hot oil or a deep fryer set to 350°F/175°C. Cook each side for 1 1⁄2 to 2 minutes or until golden brown.
  7. Cook in batches until all beignets are fried. Drain on a sheet tray with paper towels.
  8. Coat beignets heavily in honey and powdered sugar, and enjoy!
